The Different Kinds of Rubbers

An overview of the various types of rubbers used in table tennis.

Inverted (Smooth) Rubbers

The most common type of rubber, featuring a smooth surface with pimples facing inward. These rubbers excel at generating spin and are versatile for both attack and defense. Popular among modern players for their consistency and control.

Short Pips

Characterized by short, outward-facing pimples, these rubbers reduce the opponent's spin effect. They're excellent for blocking and hitting, making them popular among close-to-table attacking players.

Long Pips

Featuring longer outward-facing pimples, these rubbers are primarily defensive tools. They can reverse and disrupt incoming spin, making them challenging for opponents to read and predict.

Anti-Spin

Specially designed to nullify spin, these rubbers have very low friction surfaces. They're particularly effective for defensive players who want to return heavy topspin with minimal spin.
